This guide will help you get started with building your very own bicycle from parts! This guide will help you get all the parts and tools required. Note that this guide assumes you have some experieince tinkering with bikes!

Below you'll all the steps involved

Step 1

You will need: Frame, Fork, Headset, Rear derailleur, Brakes, Brake Levers, Shifters, Cable guide, derailleur cables, derailleur housing, cranks, bottom bracket, pedals, wheels, casseste, chain, seat post, seat, handlebars and tires

Step 2

tools needed: Lubricant, allen keys, wire cutters, wrench, screw driver

Step 3

Insert the seat and fasten it with the included clamp. Make sure you grease the part to avoid it from rusting

Step 4

Install the headset into the frame

Step 5

Attach the fork to the headset to complete the frame

Step 6

Attach the bottom bracket to the frame and add lubricant to the threads (note the drive side is reverse threaded)

Step 7

Attach the cranks to the side of the frame and tighten well

Step 8

apply lubricant to the pedals and fasten them to the cranks tightly (this will usually be a 15mm wrench)

Step 9

Attach the derailleur to the back and fsten tightly with the bolts included

Step 10

Place the chain over the crank and attach it to your derailleur (once the chain is fited make sure it rotates withough getting snaggged)

Step 11

Attach the handlebars to the fork and ensure it is tightened properly

Step 12

afix the brakes to the handlebars and ensure they are fastened tightly using the screws included

Step 13

Fit your rear brakes to the fram and ensure they are fastened tightly

Step 14

Run your break cables along the lenght of the frame and ensure they tighten when the brakes are pulled

Step 15

Attach the wheels and tires to your bike and ensure they rotate correctly with no wobbling or snagging

Step 16

Tune your derailleur (many guides for tuning can be found online, how your bike shifts gears is down to personal preference)

Step 17

Ensure you test your bike throughly beforeriding it on the street
